🏙️ Top attractions

🗼 CN Tower
The icon — glass floor, EdgeWalk, and the best view in the city.
🖼️ Royal Ontario Museum
Natural history, world cultures, and serious dinosaur skeletons.
🎨 Art Gallery of Ontario
World-class art in a Frank Gehry building.
🐟 Ripley's Aquarium
A moving walkway through a shark tunnel. Enough said.
🏒 Hockey Hall of Fame
A pilgrimage for any hockey fan.
🇨🇦 Little Canada
Canada's cities in stunning miniature.
🏰 Casa Loma
A real 1914 castle with gardens and secret passages.
🛍️ St. Lawrence Market
100+ vendors — bakers, butchers, artisans. Go hungry.
🏢 Nathan Phillips Square
The Toronto sign, events year-round, skating in winter.
🐘 Toronto Zoo
Huge zoo with a big botanical collection.

🌿 Exploring outdoors

🏝️ Toronto Islands
15-minute ferry to beaches, parks and skyline views.
🌳 High Park
Toronto's largest park — a short subway ride from Ossington Station!
🥬 Tommy Thompson Park
10 km of car-free trails on a peninsula in the lake.
🌲 Evergreen Brick Works
Weekend farmers' market + nature trails in an old brick factory.
⛰️ Scarborough Bluffs
Dramatic lakeside cliffs, beaches and trails.

🎉 Fun finds

🎨 Graffiti Alley
The famous alley of street art — free and photogenic.
🚶 PATH Network
30 km of underground walkways and 1,200 shops downtown.
👜 STACKT Market
Shipping-container market — shops, eats, events.
🌺 Allan Gardens
A glass conservatory from 1910, full of plants from everywhere.
🎪 Sankofa Square
Toronto's mini Times Square — concerts and community events.
🪖 Fort York
Original War of 1812 buildings on a 43-acre historic site.
👠 Bata Shoe Museum
4,500 years of footwear. Weirdly fascinating.
🕌 Aga Khan Museum
Stunning architecture tracing Islamic art and culture.

🚆 GO Transit — ~2–2.5 h

GO train from Union Station. When the direct Niagara train isn't running, take the Lakeshore West line to Burlington and transfer to GO Bus 12 — it drops you across from the Niagara Falls GO station. There's a GO + WEGO combo pass that includes local Niagara transit.
